# Adularescence (Moonstone Sheen)

## Overview

The signature effect of moonstone (feldspar family). On a
cabochon cut, the gem displays a soft, floating blue-to-white
sheen that seems to drift as the stone is rotated — like
moonlight on water. Mechanism: alternating thin lamellae of
orthoclase and albite (~100–300 nm each). The refractive-index
mismatch between layers scatters and interferes with light.
This is a structural optical effect, not related to color
centers or chromophore elements.


## Mechanism

Lamellar scattering + interference: alternating KAlSi₃O₈
(orthoclase) and NaAlSi₃O₈ (albite) lamellae with thickness on
the order of visible light wavelengths. Light scattered across
layers interferes coherently; blue light (shorter wavelength)
matches the layer spacing for constructive interference, producing
the blue/silver sheen. Orange and red (longer wavelengths)
interfere destructively, so the effect is always blue-white.
