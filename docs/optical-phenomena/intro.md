# Optical Phenomena

Beyond base color, gems exhibit distinct optical phenomena through internal structure. The six below are the most representative and observable during identification:

## Six Major Phenomena

| Phenomenon | One-line Summary |
|---|---|
| [Asterism (Star Effect)](asterism) | Parallel needle inclusions reflect light into star rays |
| [Chatoyancy (Cat's-Eye Effect)](chatoyancy) | Parallel fibers or tubes form a single bright band |
| [Color Change](color-change) | Gem shows distinctly different colors under different light sources |
| [Adularescence (Moonstone Sheen)](adularescence) | Layered internal structure produces soft, floating blue sheen |
| [Labradorescence (Spectrolite)](labradorescence) | Layered internal structure produces vivid metallic iridescence |
| [Aventurescence (Sunstone Glitter)](aventurescence) | Metallic platelets cause sparkly reflections inside the gem |

*See the [classification overview](../classification/intro) for the mineralogical framework.*
