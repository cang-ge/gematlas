# Asterism (Star Effect)

## Overview

When a gem contains needle- or tube-shaped inclusions aligned
along specific crystallographic directions, light reflecting
from these structures forms a 4-rayed or 6-rayed star on a
cabochon cut. The classic example is 6-rayed star ruby/sapphire
(corundum family); diopside can show a 4-rayed star. Asterism
is most visible under a single point light source; diffuse
light blurs the effect.


## Mechanism

Inclusion reflection: rutile (TiO₂) needles intersect at 60° /
120° angles aligned with the trigonal corundum c-axis. The
cabochon's domed shape guides reflections outward, optically
combining into six rays meeting at the apex.


## Example Gemstones

| Gem | Rays | Host | Needles |
|---|---|---|---|
Star Ruby / 星光红宝 | 6 | Corundum | rutile (TiO₂)
Star Sapphire / 星光蓝宝 | 6 | Corundum | rutile (TiO₂)
Star Diopside / 星光透辉石 | 4 | Diopside | magnetite
Star Rose Quartz / 星光芙蓉石 | 6 | Quartz | rutile
Star Garnet / 星光石榴石 | 4 | Garnet | rutile / apatite
