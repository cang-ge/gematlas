# Aventurescence (Sunstone Glitter)

## Overview

Aventurescence appears when a gem contains numerous small flat
metallic platelets (most commonly hematite, copper, or goethite).
Light reflecting off these platelets produces a sparkly metallic
sheen. The classic examples are:
- **Sunstone** — feldspar with hematite / goethite platelets
- **Aventurine** — quartz with chrome-mica or hematite platelets
  (green and brown varieties)
- **Imperial Porphyry** — feldspar variant
Larger, more reflective platelets produce a stronger effect.


## Mechanism

Platelet reflection: during crystallization, hematite (Fe₂O₃)
or goethite (FeO(OH)) precipitate as small hexagonal or round
platelets ~0.1–1 mm in size, oriented along specific crystal
planes. As light hits from varying angles, each platelet
sparkles in turn, producing a metallic sun-fleck effect.
Chrome-mica platelets in green aventurine reflect green light;
red/brown variants reflect from hematite.


## Example Gemstones

| Gem | Plates | Host |
|---|---|---|
Sunstone (Oregon) / 日光石 | copper / hematite | Feldspar (oligoclase / labradorite)
Aventurine Quartz (green) / 砂金石 | chrome-mica / fuchsite | Quartz
Aventurine Quartz (red-brown) / 砂金石 | hematite | Quartz
Imperial Sunstone / 帝王日光石 | large copper platelets | Feldspar
