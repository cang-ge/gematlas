# Grading

> *Carat, Color, Clarity, Cut — the four measurements that move price.*

Gem grading evaluates stones against standardized scales so buyers and sellers can compare across regions and time. The two dominant systems are the GIA 4Cs for diamonds and the hue/tone/saturation model for colored stones.

## GIA 4Cs (Diamond)

The 4Cs framework, established by the Gemological Institute of America in 1953, is the global standard for diamond grading.

### Carat (Weight)

| Unit | Grams | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1 carat (ct) | 0.2 g | Standard trade unit |
| 1 point (pt) | 0.01 ct = 0.002 g | Used for sub-carat stones |

Price increases exponentially with weight because larger rough is exponentially rarer.

D = absolutely colorless (rarest and most valuable). Z = light yellow or brown tint, still considered "normal" diamond color.

### Clarity (FL–I3 Scale)

<ClarityScale locale="en" />

Inclusions and blemishes are evaluated at 10× magnification. FL through VVS is considered eye-clean; SI shows inclusions under 10×; I shows inclusions to the naked eye.

### Cut (Quality)

Distinct from shape (round, princess, oval, etc.). Cut grade evaluates proportions, symmetry, and polish:

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Excellent** | Reflects nearly all light that enters; maximum fire and brilliance |
| **Very Good** | Reflects most light; some loss at pavilion |
| **Good** | Acceptable brilliance; some light leakage |
| **Fair** | Visible light leakage; dull appearance |
| **Poor** | Most light escapes; stone appears dark or glassy |

## Fancy Color Grading (Colored Stones)

Colored stones (Ruby, Sapphire, Emerald, etc.) use a different system based on three dimensions:

### Hue (Color Itself)

<ColorWheel locale="en" />

Hue = the basic color (red, green, blue, etc.) as positioned on the color wheel.

### Tone (Lightness)

Tone is the position on a white-to-black scale. Categorized as:

- **Very Light** (0–20% grey)
- **Light** (20–40%)
- **Medium-Light** (40–60%)
- **Medium** (60%)
- **Medium-Dark** (60–80%)
- **Dark** (80–90%)
- **Very Dark** (90–100%)

The color wheel's center disc represents tone, ranging from light at the top to dark at the bottom.

### Saturation (Intensity)

Saturation describes color purity:

- **Pastel** — soft, low chroma
- **Moderate** — clear color
- **Vivid** — strong, pure color
- **Intense** — exceptional color
- **Deep** — very dark and rich

> For most colored stones, the **vivid / intense** combination commands the highest premium. A vivid red Ruby can be worth 50× more than a dark red Ruby of equal carat weight.

## Treatment Disclosure

The Laboratory Manual Harmonisation Committee (LMHC) standardizes how treatments are disclosed in gemstone reports. Common disclosed treatments include:

| Treatment | Typical For | Disclosure |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Heat** | Ruby, Sapphire, Tanzanite | Universal acceptance |
| **Filling (oil/resin)** | Emerald | Standard for fissures |
| **HPHT** | Diamond | Required disclosure |
| **Irradiation** | Blue Topaz, some Tourmaline | Required disclosure |
| **Diffusion** | Sapphire (surface color) | Required disclosure |
| **Coating** | Various | Required disclosure |

Untreated stones command significant premiums. LMHC-recommended disclosure language: "No indications of heating" (NTE).

## Origin Premiums

For some gems, geographic origin dramatically affects value:

| Gem | Premium Origin | Standard Origin |
|-----|----------------|-----------------|
| **Ruby** | Burma (Mogok, Mong Hsu) | Mozambique, Thailand |
| **Sapphire** | Kashmir, Burma | Sri Lanka, Madagascar |
| **Emerald** | Colombia (Muzo, Chivor) | Zambia, Brazil |
| **Alexandrite** | Russia (Ural) | Brazil, Sri Lanka |
| **Paraíba Tourmaline** | Brazil (Paraíba state) | Mozambique, Nigeria |
| **Tanzanite** | Tanzania (Merelani) | (single source) |

Origin determination requires sophisticated trace-element analysis (LA-ICP-MS) and may require comparison with reference stones of known provenance.

## How to Read a Grading Report

A typical lab report includes:

1. **Identification** — species and variety
2. **Weight** — carat to 2 decimal places
3. **Dimensions** — length × width × depth (mm)
4. **Color grade** — letter (D–Z) + descriptive text for fancy
5. **Clarity grade** — FL/IF/VVS/VS/SI/I + sub-grade
6. **Cut grade** — Excellent/Very Good/Good/Fair/Poor
7. **Polish / Symmetry** — Excellent/Very Good/Good/Fair
8. **Fluorescence** — None/Faint/Medium/Strong
9. **Comments** — treatments, origin, special features
10. **Reference numbers and lab logo**
