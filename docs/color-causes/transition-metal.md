# Transition-Metal Ion Coloration

## Overview

Transition-metal ions (Cr, Fe, Mn, Co, Ni, V, Cu, Ti) have
partially filled d or f orbitals. When substituted into a host
lattice, crystal-field splitting creates d-d absorption bands
in the visible spectrum. The unabsorbed wavelengths produce
the gem's color. This is the dominant cause of color in
gemstones вЂ” roughly 70% of colored gems owe their hue to it.


## Mechanism

**Crystal-field theory**: electrostatic field from coordinating
anions (OВІвЃ», OHвЃ», FвЃ») lifts the degeneracy of the d-orbitals.
Transitions between split levels absorb specific photon
wavelengths. Coordination geometry (octahedral vs tetrahedral),
bond length, and bond angles determine the energy gap and
therefore the absorbed wavelength and resulting color.
