# Halides

## Overview

Halides are noted in gemology for color-center phenomena:
- **Fluorite** (CaF₂): exhibits multiple color-center hues
  (blue, purple, green) — the classical subject of color-center
  theory
- **Halite** (NaCl): purple variety "purple rock salt" is color-
  center produced
- **Cryolite**: rare fluoride gem


## Formula Class

MX (X = F, Cl, Br, I)

## Crystal Structure

Halide anions (F⁻, Cl⁻) bond with metal cations through ionic
interactions. Typical structures: fluorite-type (CaF₂ cubic),
halite-type (NaCl cubic). Low hardness and perfect cleavage
make halides among the most delicate gem materials.
