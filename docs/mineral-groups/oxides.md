# Oxides

## Overview

Oxides form one of the most important gem families, including
corundum (ruby, sapphire), spinel, rutile, and diaspore.
Coloring elements are predominantly transition metals (Cr, Fe,
Ti, V). Strong ionic + covalent bonding makes oxides hard
(Mohs 7–9) and durable for everyday wear.


## Formula Class

M₂O₃ / MO₂ / MO

## Crystal Structure

Oxide (O²⁻) anions form hexagonal close-packed or cubic
close-packed frameworks; metal cations occupy octahedral or
tetrahedral interstices. Corundum subgroup: trigonal; spinel
subgroup: cubic.
