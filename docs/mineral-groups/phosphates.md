# Phosphates

## Overview

Phosphates are major sources of jade-like and bio-derived gems:
- **Apatite group**: hydroxyl-, fluor-, and chlor-apatite, plus
  pyromorphite (Pb)
- **Turquoise**: copper-aluminum phosphate, porous structure
- **Lazulite**: blue phosphate often associated with turquoise
- **Brazilianite**
- **Monazite, xenotime** (radioactive Th, U, Y)


## Formula Class

PO₄³⁻ group + metal cations

## Crystal Structure

[PO₄]³⁻ tetrahedra are isolated or link via shared corners
with [OH]⁻ or [F]⁻. Metal cations (Ca, Fe, Mn, Al, Li, Be, Cu)
coordinate with P. Apatite group is hexagonal; turquoise is
triclinic.


## Representative Gem Species

| Gem | Formula | Subgroup |
|---|---|---|
| Apatite / 磷灰石 | Ca₅(PO₄)₃(F,OH,Cl) | apatite |
| Turquoise / 绿松石 | CuAl₆(PO₄)₄(OH)₈·4H₂O | turquoise |
| Lazulite / 天蓝石 | (Mg,Fe)Al₂(PO₄)₂(OH)₂ | lazulite |
| Brazilianite / 磷铝石 | NaAl₃(PO₄)₂(OH)₄ | brazilianite |
