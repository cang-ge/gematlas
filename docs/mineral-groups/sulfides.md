# Sulfides

## Overview

Sulfides are notable for their optical effects and metallic
luster:
- **Pyrite** (FeS₂): "fool's gold," cubic, often used ornamentally
- **Sphalerite** (ZnS): very high dispersion (0.156), exceeds
  diamond
- **Galena** (PbS): heavy and soft (Mohs 2.5), rarely gem-quality
- **Cinnabar** (HgS): deep red, mineralogically important


## Formula Class

M_xS_y (with S²⁻)

## Crystal Structure

Metal cations bond with S²⁻ via ionic or covalent interactions.
Common structures: NaCl-type (galena), sphalerite-type (ZnS
tetrahedral coordination), pyrite-type (FeS₂ with dumbbell
S₂²⁻ units).
