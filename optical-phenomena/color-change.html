Color Change ​

Overview ​

A few gems exhibit distinctly different hues under daylight (rich in blue-green) versus incandescent light (rich in red- yellow). The classic case is alexandrite, green in daylight and red in candlelight. Other Cr³⁺ / V³⁺-bearing species (sapphire, pyrope, certain spinels) can show similar effects. Mechanism: dual absorption bands of Cr³⁺ centered in the yellow and blue-green ranges.

Mechanism ​

Cr³⁺ exhibits two principal absorption bands: one in the yellow (~580 nm) and one in the blue (~425 nm). Under daylight (blue- green rich), yellow light is transmitted → green appearance. Under incandescent light (red-yellow rich), yellow is absorbed and blue transmission dominates, combining with the red ambient to yield red/purple. The greater the green-vs-red contrast, the higher the alexandrite rating.
